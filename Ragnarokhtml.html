Introduction

The Hunter, who spends a leisurely time hunting in the woods, travels to the world of adventure after answering calls by Tristan III, Lord of Rune-Midgards, for the recruitment of adventurers. Equipped with the ability to freely manipulate the bow, Hunters are able to hit moving targets even with eyes closed.

By installing traps that generate various effects, Hunters possess the ability to interfere with the enemy's tactics and make a mess of their operations. Aimed shooting provides ranged support, but the Hunter also has the talent to attack enemies using their own falcon, which they became familiar with during their time in the forest. The falcon is able to attack enemies with great sharpness. It is a profession that has the excellent ability to cope with all weather conditions under any circumstances. The potential builds are as follows:

  • Blitz Hunter Build: Focuses on high damage dps with a primary focus on the utilization of th falcion. This build is a fan favorite, simply due to the nature of how much damage it does while ignoring physical defense.
  • Double Strafe Build: This generic build focuses on the damage from double strafe, which is inevitably one of the most powerful pvm,mvp,and pvp skills in the game. This build primarily focuses on Dex to provide the most damage within a couple double strafes.
  • Trap Build: This is a very unique build that focuses on the hunters ability to use several different elementally charged traps. While it lacks the outright dps of other builds, it can provide for a unique hunter experience. This build is not to be underestimated.

Why Hunter?

Hunters are undeniably one of the most powerful classes in the world of Rune Midgard. Not only do they have the ability of leveling quickly and dishing out quick amounts of DPS, they are one of the most versatile classes in the game.

In contrast to other classes, hunters have the ability to pretty much level wherever they want. Whether it's in a party exploring Biolabs, or going on a solo expedition deep within clocktower; they can literally do it all.

In addition to having the versatility to level wherever they like, they are also a great hunting class (hehe ;) They level extremly quickly without the expense of spending large amounts of zeny on equips and cards. Many people will often roll a hunter class initially simply so they can generate funds for their primary class.

Leveling Grounds

While this leveling guide is primarily for Blitz Hunters, it most certainly is not limited to them.As previously stated, hunters can level in many places, regardless of their build due to their versatility. Provided is a brief outline of where I enjoy leveling. I really try to avoid Geo's though simply because of how mundane they are to hunt.

  • Level 1-30: The Newb Stage Payon Dungion/Rockers --> Mandragoras --> Mukas
  • Level 30-60: The Developing Stage Smokies/Wormtails --> Floras --> Payon Dungion level 2 --> Geographers
  • Level 60-80: The "I'm A Hunter!" Stage Geographers --> Argiopes --> Alligators --> Payon Dungion level 4 --> Clock Tower
  • Level 80-99:The Wow I am OP Stage Stings --> Chivalry 1/2 --> Turtle Dungion --> Magma Dungion --> Ice Dungion --> ANYWHERE REALLY AT THIS POINT ^__^

    The benefit of blitz hunters in later levels will allow them to quickly decimate mobs in these areas. The primary reason being that generally, these maps have mobs with very high defence and are very difficult for other classes to hunt.
